A little bit about me...

Hi, thank you for visiting my blog. My name is Chris. I'm from the Glasgow area of the U.K. and I have two beautiful daughters (6 years old and 3 years old) who are with me in the picture.... it was taken at Halloween: they don't always dress like that.

I've worked in engineering for the past 4 years, mainly in Aerospace. Currently, I'm a Product Engineer in the Automotive industry. I came into engineering with the aim of designing and producing things I could be proud of but, unfortunately, the work is mundane and unsatisfying.

Having become quite disillusioned with the engineering industry: I have decided that it is time for a career change and that a career in software development is for me. I'm of the belief that a career in this industry will allow me the independence to produce high quality work that I can be proud of and ultimately give me freedom to work on projects that interest me. Having made this decision I have began the Full Stack Developer course run by Code Institute. Unlike some of the other students taking this course: I do have a little bit of a background in software development. This was mainly in the form of using tools like MATLAB/Simulink for engineering design but the underlying principles are similar.

My short term aim is to learn how to develop software to a level where I can begin a career in the industry. My long term aim is to become a self-employed contractor and only work on projects that interest me. The aim of this blog is to chart my progress through the course and my subsequent career and provide a reference for other people who are planning on starting a career in software development and to show them that it is indeed possible to change your career whilst working in another industry.
