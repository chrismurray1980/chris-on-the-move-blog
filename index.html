A little bit about me...

Hi, thank you for visiting my blog. My name is Chris, or Christopher if you're pissed at me. I'm 38 years old and I hate my job! I'm from the Glasgow area of the U.K. and I have two beautiful daughters (6 years old and 3 years old) who are with me in the picture.... it was taken at Halloween: they don't always dress like that.

As I may have mentioned, I do genuinely hate the career path that I've chosen. I've worked in the engineering industry for the past 4 years, mainly in Aerospace, after years of study. Currently, I'm a Product Engineer in the Automotive industry. I came into engineering with the aim of designing and producing things I could be proud of but, unfortunately, the work is mundane and exasperated by dim-witted line managers with self promotion as their sole motivation in addition to the upper echelons of management being inhabited by people motivated purely by greed. This isn't always the case but tends to be the rule rather than the exception.

Having become quite disillusioned with the engineering industry: I have decided that it is time for a career change and that a career in software development is for me. I'm of the belief that a career in this industry will allow me the independence to produce high quality work that I can be proud of and ultimately give me freedom to work on projects that interest me. Having made this decision I have began the Full Stack Developer course run by Code Institute. Unlike some of the other students taking this course: I do have a little bit of a background in software development. This was mainly in the form of using tools like MATLAB/Simulink for engineering design but the underlying principles are similar.

My short term aim is to learn how to develop software to a level where I can begin a career in the industry. My long term aim is to become a self-employed contractor and only work on projects that interest me. The aim of this blog is to chart my progress through the course and my career and provide a reference for other people who are planning on starting a career in software development and to show them that it is indeed possible to change your career whilst working in another industry.

It would be quite embarrassing if it didn't work out as planned....oh well we'll see!
